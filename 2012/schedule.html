Schedule and events

Friday 19:00 - party starts!

Friday 20:00 - Demoscene documentarty (Moleman 2) + Q&A

We will show Moleman 2 - Demoscene - The Art of Algorithms, a demoscene documentary made in 2012 in english with french subtitles. After the documentary, you will be able to ask your questions about the demoscene.

Friday 22:00 - Demoshow

We will show an eclectic selection of demoscene productions (JavaScript demos, Windows demos, wild platforms...).

Saturday 14:00 - Conferences

WebGL & AudioAPI

This talk will be about webgl and webaudio. How to use them efficiently to create demo in a short period of time. provides examples of fun stuff to make. sound positionning, post processing and other cool stuff (in french and english).
by Jérôme Etienne. Jerome is a hardcore JavaScript developer. He is focused on WebGL javaScript, and cool HTML5 game stuff. He organizes various local events to spread the word in local communities. He likes tree.js quite a bit and write learningthreejs.com blog, trying to make WebGL easier to reach for beginners. He is currently writing tQuery: a thin library mixin Three.js Power with jQuery API Usability. Thus people who already knows jQuery could easily use three.js.
» http://jetienne.com/

How I learned to shrink my Javascript demos

Technical description of JS compression tricks, based on PNG-embedded source file (in french / english slides).
by Charles Boccato, computer engineer at Qobuz.com and developer since his early childhood. In the demoscene, he is known as cb from the Adinpsz demogroup and is the coder of the DemoJS 2012 invitro and the recent 'JsExe' compression tool.
» http://adinpsz.org

JSSpeccy: The toy project that wasn't

Matt will explain the challenges of writing a ZX Spectrum emulator in Javascript, and how recent developments in the language and browser technologies have turned it from a toy project into a serious rival to conventional desktop-based emulators - and how Javascript might even turn out to be the best tool for building the next generation of emulators (in english).
by Matt Wescott. Matt "Gasman" Westcott has been active in the demoscene for many years, starting out on the Spectrum, and released the first ever full-scale Javascript demo at Assembly 2004. In his day job, he's a front- and back-end developer for UK web agency Torchbox.
» http://matt.west.co.tt

Demoscene and kids

What demoscene productions you should show to your kids (in french).
by Jean-François Richard, aka JeFfR3y, old-school demoscener, co-founder of the AmigaVibes portal and member of the Vitalmotion demogroup.
» http://amigavibes.org

Demoscene history and context (short talk)

by Tristan Nitot, president of Mozilla Europe.
» http://standblog.org/blog

Openmic!

After the conferences, you will be given the opportunity to talk to the audience during ~5mn.
You can give a quick-talk about your work or production, advertise an event, ...

Saturday 20:00 - Electro/chiptune concert by DJ-PIE

DJ-PIE combines his love for gaming and electronic music to create powerful dancefloor beats making the most of the Nintendo Game Boy, despite the very tight hardware constraints.
From electro to happy hardcore, including tropical, dubstep and industrial vibes, he already seduced the crowd many times (lately in the Toy Festival in Montréal, QC, Canada).
» http://dj-pie.bandcamp.com

Saturday 22:00 - Composhow!

Saturday 23:00 - Public vote and prizegiving
